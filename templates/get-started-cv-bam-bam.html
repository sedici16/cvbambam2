What Is Cv Bam Bam?

Cv Bam Bam is a semantic CV parser built specifically for small businesses and teams. It uses AI to read your CVs, extract structured data from each one, and rank candidates by how well they match your job requirements — based on meaning, not just keywords.

Who it's for: Small business owners, founders, office managers, and team leads who handle hiring as part of their broader role — not specialist recruiters with enterprise HR systems and HR teams behind them.

What it does: Parses CVs, matches candidates to your job description semantically, exports all candidate data to CSV, and eliminates the hours-long manual screening process for every hire.

What it doesn't require: HR expertise, technical knowledge, any software setup, or a subscription commitment. To understand the technology in more depth, see our guide on what CV parsing is.

How to Use Cv Bam Bam — Step by Step

Step 1 — Go to the Tool

Navigate to /gradio. No account to create, nothing to download. The tool opens directly in your browser. You're already 30 seconds from your first result.

Step 2 — Upload Your CVs

Drag and drop your CV files into the upload area, or click to browse and select them. PDF and Word documents are both supported. Upload all your applications in one batch — you don't need to do them individually.

Step 3 — Describe What You're Looking For

Paste your job description — or a summary of the key requirements — into the text field. The AI reads this as a natural language description of the ideal candidate. It doesn't work from a keyword list; it understands what you're looking for contextually.

Step 4 — Get Your Results

Within seconds, every CV has been parsed and scored. You'll see candidates ranked by their semantic match score — highest relevance at the top. Each candidate entry includes the key data extracted from their CV: name, contact details, job history, skills, and education.

Step 5 — Export to CSV

Download your full candidate dataset as a CSV file in one click. Open it in Google Sheets or Excel. Share it with co-founders or team members. Add your own notes and scoring columns. Build your shortlist from structured, comparable data.
