The Hidden Cost of Slow Hiring — Why Every Day Counts for Small Businesses

When a role sits unfilled in a large corporation, someone else picks up the slack. In a small business, there is no slack. Every day that vacancy stays open, you are losing money — often more than you realise.

The True Cost of an Open Role

Let's put some numbers to it. If you are hiring for a role with a salary of £30,000 per year, that works out to roughly £577 per week. While that position is empty, the work that person would be doing is either not getting done, or it is being done badly by someone who already has a full plate.

The costs stack up fast:

  • Lost productivity — the work simply is not getting done, which means missed deadlines, slower service, or lost sales
  • Overworked team — your existing staff are covering the gap, which leads to overtime costs, mistakes, and eventually burnout and resignations
  • Missed revenue — if the role is customer-facing or revenue-generating, every week without someone in post is money you will never recover
  • Your own time — as the business owner, every hour you spend on hiring is an hour you are not spending on growth, clients, or operations

A conservative estimate puts the cost of an unfilled role at £500 to £1,000 per week for a typical small business position. For more senior or specialist roles, it can be significantly higher.

How Long Does Hiring Actually Take?

In the UK, the average time-to-hire — from posting the job to the candidate starting — is between 27 and 36 days. For small businesses without dedicated HR, it is often longer. Some roles drag on for two or three months.

Where does all that time go? The breakdown typically looks like this:

  • Writing and posting the job advert: 1-3 days
  • Collecting applications: 7-14 days
  • Screening CVs: 5-10 days (this is the bottleneck)
  • Scheduling and conducting interviews: 7-14 days
  • Making an offer and notice period: 7-30 days

Notice where the biggest controllable delay is? CV screening. It sits right in the middle of the process, and for most small businesses, it is the step that takes longest because it requires the most manual effort. We break this down further in our guide on how long hiring should take.

The Cost Comparison: Three Ways to Hire

When you need to fill a role, you broadly have three options. Here is what each one actually costs:

Option 1: Use a recruitment agency. Recruiters typically charge 15% to 25% of the candidate's first-year salary. For a £30,000 role, that is £4,500 to £7,500. For a £50,000 role, you are looking at £7,500 to £12,500. You get convenience, but at a serious cost — especially if you are hiring multiple roles per year.

Option 2: Do it yourself, manually. The job advert itself might cost £100-300 on a job board. But the real cost is your time. If you spend 20 hours screening CVs, conducting interviews, and managing the process — and your time is worth £50 per hour to the business — that is £1,000 in time alone, plus the weeks of delay while you fit it around your other responsibilities.

Option 3: Do it yourself, with AI screening. An AI screening tool like Cv Bam Bam costs between £0 and £29 per month. It handles the most time-consuming step — reading and ranking all those CVs — in seconds. Your total time investment drops dramatically because you are only reviewing the top candidates, not wading through hundreds of applications.

Where AI Screening Cuts the Bottleneck

The reason AI screening has such a dramatic impact on time-to-hire is that it targets the single slowest step in the process. Screening is where hiring stalls for small businesses — not because the task is complex, but because the volume is overwhelming and there is nobody dedicated to doing it.

With AI screening, what used to take 5-10 days takes minutes. You upload your CVs, get a ranked shortlist, and move straight to interviews. For many small businesses, this cuts total time-to-hire by 50% to 80%.

Let's translate that back into money. If you shave two weeks off your hiring process for a £30,000 role, you save roughly £1,150 in lost productivity alone — before accounting for the value of your own time and the reduced risk of losing your best candidates to faster-moving competitors.

The Case for Investing in Faster Screening

When you are running a small business, every cost feels significant. Spending money on a hiring tool when you could "just do it yourself" feels like an unnecessary expense. But consider the maths:

  • A recruitment agency costs thousands per hire
  • Manual screening costs weeks of your time and risks missing the best candidates
  • An AI screening tool costs less than a single hour of your time per month — and pays for itself on the first hire

Slow hiring is not just inconvenient. It is expensive. Every day a role sits unfilled, you are paying for it — in lost output, in team morale, in missed opportunities. The fastest way to stop paying that cost is to eliminate the bottleneck. And for most small businesses, that bottleneck is CV screening.
