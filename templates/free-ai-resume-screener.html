What Makes This Different From Traditional Resume Screening

Most automated resume screening tools work on a simple principle: scan CVs for the keywords in your job description and filter out anything that doesn't match. The problem is that language doesn't work that way. Candidates describe the same experience in different words.

A candidate who writes "led a cross-functional team of eight people to deliver a complex software product on time and under budget" has exactly the team management and project delivery experience you're looking for — even if they never used the exact phrase "team management." A keyword filter would reject them. Cv Bam Bam's semantic AI would rank them highly.

This isn't a marginal improvement. Research suggests that keyword-based systems reject up to 75% of qualified candidates. When you're receiving twenty or thirty applications for a role, that means you might be interviewing from the wrong half of your applicant pool.
