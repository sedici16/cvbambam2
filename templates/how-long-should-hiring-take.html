How Long Should Hiring Take for a Small Business?

Reading time: 5 minutes  |  Topic: Small Business Hiring

Time-to-hire is one of the most important hiring metrics — and one of the most overlooked by small businesses. Take too long and your best candidates accept offers elsewhere. Move too fast and you make a costly mistake. So what's the right target?

The Benchmarks

According to 2026 research, large companies fill roles in around 28 days on average. Small businesses take 36.5 days. When the process stretches past 40 days, candidate drop-off rates increase by 12% — meaning you lose strong applicants simply because you moved too slowly.

A reasonable target for a small business is 3 to 4 weeks from posting to offer for most roles. Senior or specialist positions may take 5 to 6 weeks. Anything beyond that is worth examining.

Where Small Businesses Lose Time

  • CV screening — reading every application manually, especially when there are 30+
  • Slow decisions between stages — candidates wait a week between interview and feedback
  • Too many interview rounds — three rounds is rarely necessary for a small business hire
  • Delayed reference checks — leaving these to the last minute adds days
  • Unclear decision-making — not knowing who has final say causes internal delays

A Realistic Timeline
