Lightweight ATS Alternatives for Teams That Don't Need Enterprise Software

Reading time: 8 minutes  |  Topic: Hiring Tools for Small Business

Enterprise applicant tracking systems are built for companies that hire hundreds of people per year. They're powerful, expensive, and complex — three qualities that make them the wrong choice for most small businesses. But the alternative to a full ATS isn't manual chaos. There are lightweight approaches that give you the organisation and efficiency you need without the overhead you don't.

This guide explains why enterprise ATS is overkill for small teams and walks through the lightweight alternatives that actually work.

Why Enterprise ATS Is Overkill for Most Small Businesses

Applicant tracking systems were built for enterprise HR departments managing high-volume, complex hiring across multiple locations, departments, and job categories. They're designed to handle:

  • Hundreds of open roles simultaneously
  • Thousands of applications per month
  • Teams of recruiters with different access levels
  • Complex compliance and documentation requirements
  • Integration with payroll, HRIS, and onboarding systems

Most small businesses have one or two open roles at a time, receive twenty to fifty applications per role, and have one or two people involved in hiring decisions. The full ATS feature set is irrelevant — and the cost is real. Enterprise platforms commonly charge £300–£2,000 per month, billed annually.

What a Small Business Actually Needs From a Hiring Tool

Strip away the enterprise features and the core requirements for a small business hiring process are simple:

  • Collect applications through one channel
  • Screen CVs without reading every word manually
  • Track which stage each candidate is at
  • Export or share candidate data for team review
  • No complex onboarding, no annual contracts

This is a much simpler requirements list. And it has much simpler solutions.

Category 1 — Simple Kanban-Based Hiring Trackers

Tools like Trello, Notion, and ClickUp offer free kanban board templates designed for hiring pipeline management. Each candidate becomes a card that moves through columns: Applied → Screening → Phone Screen → Interview → Offer → Hired.

Best for: tracking candidate stages and collaborating with team members on hiring decisions.

Gap: these tools don't parse CVs or screen candidates. You still need to read every application manually before you can move candidates into the pipeline. They solve the tracking problem, not the screening problem.

Category 2 — Lightweight Cloud ATS

There's a growing category of SMB-focused ATS products positioned between enterprise systems and manual processes. These typically offer careers page hosting, application collection, basic CV parsing, pipeline management, and team collaboration tools — at lower price points than enterprise alternatives.

Best for: businesses hiring 10+ people per year who want structured pipeline management built in.

Gap: even lightweight ATS tools usually require subscription fees and carry the keyword-matching problem — their parsing is typically not semantic, so qualified candidates still get filtered incorrectly. Category 3 — CV Parsers + Spreadsheets (The Recommended Approach)

The most effective lightweight approach for most small businesses combines two simple tools: a semantic CV parser and a spreadsheet.

The workflow:

  • Receive CVs by email
  • Upload to a semantic CV parser (batch upload)
  • Parser extracts data and ranks candidates by match to your job description
  • Export all candidate data to CSV in one click
  • Import into Google Sheets: add columns for stage, interviewer notes, decisions
  • Share sheet with anyone involved in the decision

This approach gives you the automated screening of an ATS (without the keyword-only limitation), the pipeline tracking of a kanban tool (in a spreadsheet), and the collaboration features you need — all in a stack that costs nothing and requires zero configuration.

When to Graduate to a Real ATS

There are signals that indicate a full ATS investment is justified:

  • You have a dedicated HR person who will own and configure the system
  • You're hiring more than 20 people per month consistently
  • You have complex compliance requirements that demand documented audit trails
  • You're managing multiple open roles with separate hiring teams simultaneously

Until those thresholds apply, the lightweight stack is the better choice.
